01 · The Person

Who is
Abuela Matilde?

Matilde Cañada Gutiérrez is not just the name of a brand. She is the woman who held an entire family together when she was still a child — and whose quiet generosity made everything that followed possible.

Matilde was the eldest of five siblings: Matilde, María Dolores, Angustias, Inmaculada, and Manuel. From a very young age, with a mother in poor health, she took on the role of caregiver for all of them. It was not a choice; it was a vocation she embraced as naturally as an olive tree embraces the earth.

Her father Fernando was an entrepreneurial man with great vision, who spent his life building a legacy that today forms the foundation of this project. Together with Matilde and José, he planted the seed of the organic olive grove that years later would bear fruit in the first harvest of the Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde.

Fernando Carpio Cañada, Matilde's son and José's brother, is a co-founder and a fundamental pillar of the project. His active involvement bridges two generations and makes Abuela Matilde a truly family endeavour in every sense of the word.

Today, the vision of Francisco — Inmaculada's son — together with Fernando and José, Matilde's sons, carries forward the legacy of grandfather Fernando: to offer the finest product possible at a fair price. A promise so simple it is almost revolutionary in a market that too often chooses shortcuts.

02 · The People of the Field

The Quesada family
and Juan Casado of Gesmontes

Behind every bottle of Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde are real people giving their very best. An olive grove does not care for itself — it takes hands, knowledge, and a true vocation.

The Quesada family is one of the fundamental pillars of the family agricultural enterprise. José Quesada and his brothers Paco, Antonio, and Luis are responsible for the day-to-day management of the grove. They are the ones who know each olive tree intimately, who decide the exact moment of harvest, and who ensure that every olive arrives at the mill in perfect condition.

Their work is the invisible foundation on which all the quality of the product rests. Without them, there is no Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde. They are an essential part of the project, and their commitment to excellence in the field is what makes excellence in the bottle possible.

Juan Casado, agronomist at Gesmontes (Córdoba), is the architect of the grove's organic management. Gesmontes is a company specialised in organic olive grove management and in developing their own range of care and nutrition products for organic olive cultivation, elaborated in Córdoba using a methodology that is radically different from conventional practice.

Where the traditional approach prescribes chemical products to treat symptoms — as happens in so many fields — Juan works by strengthening the tree from within: the soil as a living ecosystem, the tree as an organism in balance. The oil as the natural consequence of that deep care.

03 · Origin

A place chosen
since the Roman Empire

Torredonjimeno, in the heart of the Jaén countryside, is marked on Vatican maps as an exceptionally favourable zone for olive cultivation since Roman times. It is no coincidence: it is geography, history, and vocation.

The Carpio-Cañada family has been cultivating this same land for at least four generations. A rain-fed grove laid out in a traditional 12×12-metre framework, with large multi-trunk olive trees — some centuries old — and the Picual variety at its finest.

A farm in Torredonjimeno, just a few kilometres from Córdoba, where for miles around there is no polluting industry to be found. Only olive trees — an immense green sea covering the plains all the way to the horizon.

The Picual variety of Jaén is not simply the most widely planted in the world. On its home soil, under full sun and in clay-rich terrain, it reaches an organoleptic complexity few regions can match: intense green fruitiness, notes of tomato and freshly cut grass, and a bitterness and pepperiness that signal health, not defect.

28 hectares of olive trees tended with a full awareness of what they represent: not merely a crop, but a living heritage. The decision to transition to organic farming, made six years ago, was not welcomed by everyone. Today, some of those who once criticised the path are reconsidering their own.

04 · Why Premium

An oil that deserves
to be called Premium

"Premium" is not a marketing label. It is the result of every decision made in the grove, the mill, and the bottle. These are the reasons why the Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde is different.

🍂 Early Harvest

The olives are harvested between late October and early November, depending on the optimal ripeness of each season. Harvesting at exactly the right moment is the very first decision that defines the quality of the oil.

🖐 100% Tree-Picked · Traditional Method

Olives are gathered by hand using the traditional method with nets and poles, supported by tractors. No intensive or super-intensive harvesting machinery. Every olive arrives at the mill undamaged, clean, and free of mud.

💧 Rain-Fed Grove · No Artificial Irrigation

Traditional 12×12-metre framework. Rainwater only. The trees work under natural conditions, developing an olive of concentration and complexity that irrigated farming simply cannot replicate.

⚡ From Grove to Bottle on the Same Day

The oil is produced on the same day as the harvest and bottled at the same facility. Cold mechanical extraction by expert master millers. No waiting, no oxidation, no compromise.

🌱 Organic Products Only · Gesmontes

The grove is nourished exclusively with organic products developed in Córdoba by Gesmontes, specialists in organic olive grove management. No chemicals, no shortcuts.

❤️ The Care of the People

The Quesada family in the field. Juan Casado managing the agronomy. Master millers in the extraction. Every person involved contributes their knowledge and dedication. That cannot be manufactured — it must be cultivated.

A bold oil. A slight greenish hue. Intense flavour and the true texture of olive juice extracted at the precise moment of ripeness. Like a great wine, caring for the raw material at every stage of the process yields an oil of the finest quality.

05 · The Story

The day of the mud
and the premium olive

Sometimes great projects are born from a moment of quiet outrage. This was ours.

We arrived at the cooperative with a trailer of perfect olives: 100% tree-picked, free of soil, at their optimal point of ripeness. The result of hours of careful work in the field. Olives that barely needed cleaning.

Arriving right behind us in the queue was another trailer. Full of mud, soil, and leaves. Both batches of olives — ours and the muddy one — would end up mixed together in the same hopper, in the same oil, under the same cooperative label.

If you give the best of your land and the best of your care, it deserves an oil that reflects that. Not one that dilutes it.

— José Carpio, co-founder of Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde

That day the idea was born. Separate our olives. Take them to a trusted mill that worked to the same standards. Control the process from tree to bottle. Extract the finest oil possible from our olives — not the average oil of an uncontrolled blend.

That is how Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde came to be.

06 · The First Client

A conversation
in Lausanne

The finest endorsement for a premium product is when someone who truly understands markets, branding, and quality believes in it before it even exists.

Dan Witting is the co-founder of Baabuk, an international sustainable footwear brand present in more than 40 countries, alongside his wife Galina. Before Baabuk, Dan spent over ten years in marketing at Philip Morris, developing a sharp instinct for product and positioning.

During a conversation in Lausanne, Canton of Vaud (Switzerland), Dan asked a direct question: "Do you have your own oil?" The answer was no. All the family's production went to the cooperative. It was Dan who saw, before we did, the potential of what we had.

Dan became our first client. The first Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde we produced outside the cooperative — following the tradition of the maquila, the oil kept for the producer's own household — went largely to him. To his table in Switzerland.

Six years on, Dan is still a client. Not because of any contract, but because the oil speaks for itself. That is the kind of loyalty that builds a real brand.

07 · Founding Values

Manuel José Carpio Bueno
The craftsman who taught us everything

Behind every authentic brand there is an ethic. Ours has a name and a face.

Manuel José Carpio Bueno was a carpenter his entire life in Torredonjimeno. He began making furniture in the courtyard of his home and went on to build the office of Doña Elena Salgado, then Director General of Communications, in the former Palacio de Telecomunicaciones in Madrid — today the city's town hall. From a neighbourhood workshop to the heart of the State.

What made that journey possible was not advertising or marketing. It was word of mouth. The children of his clients came back when they needed furniture of their own. Because Manuel kept his promises, delivered more than he was paid for, and never sacrificed quality for price.

Being a good person is, in the long run, good business.

— Manuel José Carpio Bueno, carpenter and farmer

This phrase sums up the philosophy of Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde. We are not looking for today's customer. We are looking for the customer who stays — and whose children come back after them. That is the business model our father taught us, and it is the one we apply to every bottle that leaves Torredonjimeno.

08 · Market Context

Premium EVOO worldwide:
a category in full expansion

Superior quality extra virgin olive oil is no longer a niche. It is an accessible luxury category growing rapidly in sophisticated markets — and the Latin American premium segment is only just beginning.

The Evooleum Guide — an international reference with 27 judges from 9 countries — confirms year after year that Spain produces the world's finest EVOOs. The Picual variety from Jaén is a consistent presence at the very top. The discerning consumer in Mexico City, São Paulo, Buenos Aires, or Bogotá increasingly understands the difference between an ordinary oil and a truly exceptional one.

🌿 Organic & Clean Label Trend

The Latin American premium consumer prioritises traceability, clean ingredients, and verifiable provenance. A certified organic Premium EVOO answers exactly that demand.

🏆 Accessible Luxury

Premium EVOO is positioned as a corporate gift, a fine dining ingredient, and a symbol of sophisticated lifestyle. Average price: €15–30 per 500ml in the European market.

📖 Storytelling with a Real Story

The brands that succeed in this segment have an authentic human story behind them. Abuela Matilde has one: family, territory, legacy, and an organic conversion born of genuine conviction.

🌎 The Latin American Opportunity

Spain and its quality products enjoy enormous prestige across Latin America. Artisan Spanish Premium Organic EVOO has a positioning opportunity equivalent to boutique Chilean or Argentine wine in Europe.

09 · Milestones

From the cooperative
to the international market

Generations ago

The Carpio-Cañada family has its grove integrated within the local cooperative of Torredonjimeno. Several family members hold board positions. The oil goes into the common blend.

~2019 — The mud day

A delivery at the cooperative reveals the senselessness of mixing premium olives with low-quality ones. The decision to produce independently — the Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde — is born.

~2020 — Start of the organic transition

Matilde and Fernando (grandfather) open the path through their generosity. The family unanimously agrees to begin the organic conversion alongside Juan Casado (Gesmontes, Córdoba). Fernando Carpio Cañada and José lead the change together with the Quesada family in the field.

First year — The Swiss maquila

In Lausanne, Canton of Vaud, Dan Witting — Swiss entrepreneur and co-founder of Baabuk — becomes the first client of the Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde. Most of that first production crosses the Alps. Word of mouth begins.

2025–2026 — Brand development

Trademark application for "AOVE Bio Abuela Matilde" (ref. M4347748) filed with the Spanish Patent and Trademark Office (OEPM). Business structuring, visual identity, and opening towards new international markets.

2026 — Latin America horizon

With a consolidated base in Europe, distribution agreements for the Premium Organic EVOO Abuela Matilde are being explored across Latin America: fine dining, premium retail, luxury hospitality, and specialist channels.
